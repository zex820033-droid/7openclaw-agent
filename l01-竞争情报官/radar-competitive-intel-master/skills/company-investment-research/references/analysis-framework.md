# Investment Analysis Framework Reference

This document provides detailed frameworks and methodologies for conducting investment research.

## Moat Analysis Framework

### Types of Economic Moats

1. **Network Effects**
   - Value increases as more users join
   - Examples: Payment platforms, social networks, marketplaces
   - Measurement: User growth rate, engagement metrics, retention

2. **Switching Costs**
   - High cost/friction to change to competitor
   - Examples: Enterprise software, banking, healthcare IT
   - Measurement: Customer retention rate, contract lengths, migration complexity

3. **Cost Advantages**
   - Structural cost advantages over competitors
   - Sources: Scale, proprietary process, preferential access
   - Measurement: Gross margin vs peers, unit economics

4. **Intangible Assets**
   - Patents, brands, regulatory licenses, data
   - Examples: Pharmaceuticals, luxury brands, utilities
   - Measurement: Patent portfolio, brand value, regulatory barriers

5. **Efficient Scale**
   - Market only supports one or few profitable firms
   - Examples: Utilities, infrastructure, niche markets
   - Measurement: Market size vs minimum efficient scale

### Moat Assessment Criteria

**Width (Competitive Advantage Period):**
- Narrow: 0-5 years
- Medium: 5-10 years
- Wide: 10+ years

**Trend:**
- Widening: Competitive advantages strengthening
- Stable: Maintaining current position
- Narrowing: Competitive threats increasing

## Financial Analysis Metrics

### Profitability Metrics

- **Gross Margin:** (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue
  - Industry comparison critical
  - Trend indicates pricing power

- **Operating Margin:** Operating Income / Revenue
  - Reflects operational efficiency
  - Target: Above industry median

- **Net Margin:** Net Income / Revenue
  - Bottom-line profitability
  - Watch for one-time items

- **Return on Equity (ROE):** Net Income / Shareholders' Equity
  - Quality of earnings metric
  - Target: >15% for quality companies

- **Return on Invested Capital (ROIC):** NOPAT / Invested Capital
  - Capital efficiency measure
  - Target: ROIC > WACC

### Growth Metrics

- **Revenue CAGR:** Compound annual growth rate
  - 5-year historical vs forward projections
  - Compare to industry and GDP growth

- **EPS Growth:** Earnings per share growth rate
  - Should track or exceed revenue growth
  - Watch for buyback-driven growth

- **Free Cash Flow Growth:** Operating cash flow - CapEx
  - More reliable than earnings
  - Enables dividends, buybacks, debt reduction

### Efficiency Metrics

- **Asset Turnover:** Revenue / Total Assets
  - How efficiently assets generate revenue
  - Higher is better, industry-dependent

- **Inventory Turnover:** COGS / Average Inventory
  - Inventory management efficiency
  - Industry-specific benchmarks

- **Days Sales Outstanding (DSO):** (Accounts Receivable / Revenue) × 365
  - Collection efficiency
  - Lower is better

## Valuation Methodologies

### Relative Valuation

**P/E Ratio (Price-to-Earnings):**
- Current P/E vs historical 5-year average
- Current P/E vs peer group median
- Consider: PEG ratio for growth adjustment

**EV/EBITDA (Enterprise Value to EBITDA):**
- Useful for capital-intensive businesses
- Less affected by capital structure
- Compare to industry median

**P/S Ratio (Price-to-Sales):**
- Useful for high-growth, pre-profit companies
- Less manipulable than earnings
- Industry-specific benchmarks

**P/B Ratio (Price-to-Book):**
- Relevant for asset-heavy businesses
- Banking, real estate sectors
- Target: P/B < 1.5 for value

### Absolute Valuation

**Discounted Cash Flow (DCF):**
1. Project free cash flows (5-10 years)
2. Determine terminal value
3. Select appropriate discount rate (WACC)
4. Calculate present value
5. Apply margin of safety (20-30%)

**Key Inputs:**
- Revenue growth assumptions
- Operating margin assumptions
- Capital expenditure requirements
- Working capital changes
- Terminal growth rate (typically 2-3%)
- Discount rate (WACC typically 8-12%)

### Margin of Safety

**Calculation:**
- Intrinsic Value - Current Price) / Intrinsic Value

**Guidelines:**
- Conservative investors: 30-40% margin
- Moderate investors: 20-30% margin
- Aggressive investors: 10-20% margin

## Growth Analysis Framework

### Growth Drivers Checklist

**Product Innovation:**
- New product pipeline
- R&D productivity (revenue/R&D spend)
- Time to market vs competitors
- Product lifecycle stage

**Market Expansion:**
- Geographic expansion opportunities
- New customer segments
- Market penetration rate
- TAM vs current revenue

**Pricing Power:**
- Historical pricing trends
- Pass-through of cost increases
- Premium vs discount positioning
- Brand strength

**M&A Activity:**
- Acquisition track record
- Integration success rate
- ROI on acquisitions
- Pipeline opportunities

### Sustainability Assessment

**Ask:**
- Is growth organic or acquisition-driven?
- Are margins expanding or contracting with growth?
- Is growth capital-efficient (low CapEx/Revenue)?
- Can competitors easily replicate?

## Risk Assessment Framework

### Risk Categories

**Business Risks:**
- Customer concentration
- Supplier dependence
- Technology obsolescence
- Regulatory changes
- Competitive intensity

**Financial Risks:**
- Leverage (Debt/EBITDA > 3x concerning)
- Interest coverage (EBIT/Interest < 3x concerning)
- Liquidity (Current ratio < 1.5 concerning)
- Covenant compliance

**Management Risks:**
- Track record
- Compensation alignment
- Related party transactions
- Communication transparency

**Market Risks:**
- Cyclicality
- Currency exposure
- Commodity exposure
- Geographic concentration

### Red Flags

- Declining gross margins
- Revenue growth without cash flow growth
- Frequent accounting changes
- High executive turnover
- Aggressive accounting policies
- Large goodwill/intangibles (>40% assets)
- Persistent working capital increases
- Serial acquisitions without integration success

## Investment Decision Framework

### BUY Criteria
1. Strong competitive moat (wide and widening)
2. Capable management with aligned interests
3. Growth runway of 5+ years
4. ROIC > 15% and > WACC
5. Trading at 20%+ discount to intrinsic value
6. Limited downside risks
7. Positive industry tailwinds

### HOLD Criteria
1. Fairly valued (±10% of intrinsic value)
2. Maintaining competitive position
3. Moderate growth prospects
4. No material deterioration in fundamentals
5. Portfolio diversification benefits

### SELL Criteria
1. Overvalued (>20% above intrinsic value)
2. Moat deteriorating
3. Fundamental business deterioration
4. Better opportunities available
5. Original investment thesis invalidated
6. Risk profile materially increased

## Industry-Specific Considerations

### Technology
- Focus: R&D intensity, patent quality, developer ecosystem
- Key metrics: ARR, net retention, gross margin
- Risks: Rapid obsolescence, competition

### Consumer
- Focus: Brand strength, customer loyalty, pricing power
- Key metrics: Same-store sales, market share, brand value
- Risks: Consumer sentiment, competition, regulation

### Healthcare
- Focus: Pipeline, patent cliff, regulatory approval
- Key metrics: R&D productivity, drug portfolio, patent life
- Risks: Regulatory, pricing pressure, patent expiry

### Financials
- Focus: Asset quality, capital adequacy, loan portfolio
- Key metrics: ROE, NPL ratio, capital ratios, NIM
- Risks: Credit quality, interest rate, regulation

### Industrials
- Focus: Order backlog, cycle position, operational leverage
- Key metrics: ROIC, margin trajectory, capacity utilization
- Risks: Cyclicality, commodity prices, competition
